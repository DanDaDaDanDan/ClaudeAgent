# ABOUT THE UNIVERSE

## The Panopticon of Veridia

**THE SETTING**

**Veridia** - A sterile, walled utopia of white towers and omnipresent surveillance. Citizens are genetically perfected but emotionally suppressed. Death is bureaucratic; identity is data. Every citizen is backed up in the Helix Vault, their memories and personalities stored for potential restoration.

**The Ex-Zone** - Outside the walls lies a chaotic, mutagenic jungle. Here, the failed experiments live—those who rejected Veridia's order or were rejected by it. The environment is hostile and sentient, growing structures of flesh and chitin, beautiful and terrible.

**The Border** - The fortified zone where these two realities clash. The Wall is constantly being consumed by Ex-Zone growth, constantly being repaired. Guards patrol, enhanced with Ex-Zone symbionts that give them abilities—and complications.

**THE TECHNOLOGY**

All technology in Veridia is *bio-tech*: grown rather than manufactured. Doors are sphincters. Data is stored in blood and tissue. Devices are made of flesh or insects. The line between tool and organism does not exist.

**THE SECRET**

Veridia operates on *The Loop*—a generational reset that recycles the same souls into new cloned bodies, selectively preserving and erasing memories. This has been happening for centuries. Some remember. Most do not.
